Irezumi (入れ墨, 入墨, 紋身, 刺花, 剳青, 黥 or 刺青) is a Japanese word that refers to the insertion of ink under the skin to leave a permanent, usually decorative mark; a form of tattooing.

How does it work?
1. You choose or design a picture.
2. Body Printer paints it in your skin with erasable ink.
3. Once you decide to tatoo it, full color technology starts to work for you.

As when do it manually, the printer allows you to rest while, automatically, paints beautiful photorealistic images under your skin.
